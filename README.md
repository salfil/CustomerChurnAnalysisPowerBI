This is a customer churn analysis dashboard that was built to understand the current churn rate of our customers and how aspects such as their contract type, services they were using, relationship status and others impacted churn rate as well as revenue. Loom video below:

https://www.loom.com/share/f046d1870c0e4b5d81e00b1077f6b875

Skills and Methodology:

- Power BI was used to conduct this analytics, which involved calculating the relevant metrics such as the churn rate, total number of customers, average tenure of customers etc. 
- Multiple Power BI visualisations such as bar charts were used to show data across different categories and their impact on customer churn. 

Results

- Customer Churn was higher than predicted by 4%
- Customers that stay for longer churn less, with the highest churn rate being for customers who only stay for the 0-10 month mark.
- Churn rates for monthly plans (41%) were more than double that of one-year plans (15%) and more than 20 times that of 2 year plans (1.5%). Monthly plans also account for 56% of our customer base.
- The churn rate amongst senior citizens (43%) was almost double that of non senior citizens (23%)
- Customers who never had online services such as online security and backup were twice as likely to churn that customers without them.
- Monthly charges didn't have an impact on the churn rate.
- Customers on the Fiber Optic were more than twice as likely to churn that customers with DSL. The average total charge for customers on Fiber Optic was 50% more than customers on DSL. 

Recommendations:

- Incentivise customers that are currently on a month to month plan to a yearly plan through extra benefits, discounts etc.
- If customers are unable to switch from month to month to yearly plans, incentivise longer tenures through rewards at certain milestones such as the 6 month/12 month mark.
- Include free trials of the online services to get customers trying the online security and backup, as this could incentivise them to switch to plans that include this and therefore decrease churn.
- As the churn for Fiber optic users was quite high, we should look into the current quality of the Fiber Optic services and if there's any way we can improve it for customers to prevent churn.
